Why We Need to Champion Nokia

The pioneering spirit that shaped mobile communication deserves recognition

Legendary Durability

Nokia phones were legendary for their indestructible build quality. The Nokia 3310 could survive drops, water, and even construction work. They set the standard for mobile phone reliability that modern smartphones struggle to match.

Built to Last Forever

Incredible Battery Life

Nokia phones had batteries that lasted for days, even weeks. The 3310 could go up to 2 weeks on a single charge with its revolutionary power management. Today's smartphones struggle to last a full day.

Weeks of Power

Innovation Pioneer

Nokia pioneered mobile gaming with Snake, brought cameras to phones, created the first smartphone, and established many mobile communication standards. They shaped the mobile industry we know today.

Nokia's Legendary Journey

From a humble paper mill to mobile revolution - 158 years of innovation

1865

üè≠ Foundation

Nokia was founded as a paper mill company by Fredrik Idestam in Tampere, Finland. The company name comes from the Nokianvirta river (Nokia River) where the original mill was located.

1967

üîÑ Modern Nokia

Nokia Corporation was formed through the merger of Nokia Ab, Finnish Rubber Works, and Finnish Cable Works, creating a diversified industrial conglomerate.

1987

üì± First Mobile

Nokia launched the Mobira Cityman 900, one of the first handheld mobile phones, weighing just 800g and costing $4,560 in today's money.

1994

üöÄ Mobile Revolution

Nokia launched the 2100 series, marking the beginning of their dominance in mobile phones with the iconic Nokia tune and revolutionary design.

2000

üëë Peak Success

The legendary Nokia 3310 was released - the most durable phone ever made, featuring the iconic Snake game and selling over 126 million units worldwide.

2007

‚ö° iPhone Challenge

Apple launched the iPhone, beginning the smartphone revolution that would challenge Nokia's dominance and reshape the entire mobile industry.

2014

üîÑ Microsoft Era

Nokia's mobile division was acquired by Microsoft for $7.2 billion, marking the end of an era for the Finnish mobile phone pioneer.

2016

üéØ New Beginning

Nokia returned to mobile phones through HMD Global, bringing back the beloved brand with pure Android experience and classic Nokia reliability.
